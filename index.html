Your Data.
Working For Us.

A digital footprint, the trace left for every action taken on the internet, every search, every click, every tap every millisecond spent on a page. Simply the sum of actions that you've made ever since you first made an account or agreed to terms you never read or could read.

This footprint, your history, is data and data is worth money, individually not much and in quantity it is worth a lot. The data brokerage industry generates over $200 billion annually by packing your habits, health and location to the highest bidder. Most often the bidders are advertisers, insurance companies, employers and governments.
Services aren't free, even if the cost isn't shown upfront, for often the cost is you.

This site, is simply an example of what data you give away by simply being on it, and what might be done with such data.
Now, don't you wonder what information you've given and what that information is worth?

Where Your Data Goes

// Projected trajectory based on current industry trends.

2025

COLLECTION

Google process over 8.5 billion searches a day "don't be evil" (old Google motto, dropped in 2018). Meta tracks you across 8 million websites through embedded pixels "move fast and break things" (Zuckerberg/Meta motto). Account or not.
Amazon knows what you almost bought, what is likely that you will buy. Data brokers like Acxiom hold an average of 1,500 data points per person.
This is but the current market.

2030

CONSOLIDATION

AI companies have taken in decades of human posts, chat logs, behaviour and history, all to predict you as person, to know what you will do before even you yourself will know.

After all "nothing to hide, nothing to fear" friends.

Governments purchase data in tons, no warrant required.
The health insurance already knows all risks and can set almost any rate they see fit, simply based of your grocery bill.

2035

SATURATION

Personalized advertisement, the pain of consumers and the scheme of capitalism that is there to make you work till you break.
The algorithm it doesn't just respond to what you want, no no no, it tells you what you want.
Products to manage stress, products to help with sleep, information that is based on your relationships sold to you before you even bring out your wallet.

Retirement? Please. With this direction of Hyper-consumerism it is but a joke.

2045

CONTROL

Ah yes, a mobility credit score, a system that judges you for every choice every word, every action.
It tells you where you can live.
It tells you how you can live.
It tells you if you can sleep.
It tells you if you can eat.
Travel? What travel?

Any choice, any comment made of frustration, any joke said between friends would become the damocles that leads to your life's execution.
Maybe not literally, but certainly it would damn you for the rest of your years.
Appeals? you'd be lucky if they exist, never the less that they even go anywhere.

Your score, is your life.

Now What?

What can be done? Oh what can be done? A circus is what this has become.
For now you still have some control of your information.
So delete what you can, audit what you can't. Understand the risk and choose what you leave behind, use a privacy focused software, use a DNS blocker.
Opt out of data broker listings where you can, it takes maybe an hour but can save you years.
You can't erase yourself from the internet of course, but at least you might have a bit more control of what others know of you.

Data is already out there, that won't change. Yet now the only question is whether it is worth something to you?
